Expected Values and Variation

Quarantine fails to prevent significant mortalities when quarantine effectiveness does not reach the 80% threshhold. Toggle off the first two strategies (orange and blue) to view the more effective strategies: Travel restrictions have only modest effects, while countrywide vaccination shows the lowest overall mortality and lowest variability in mortality. Note the relatively fat tails for both localized vaccination strategies. Travel restrictions on top of countrywide vaccination make no difference in the model so those results have been omitted from the discussion.

Below is the expected values of the epidemic's progression. Toggle off the two ineffective strategies to view the effective interventions.

Ineffective quarantine only prolongs the epidemic. Localized vaccination limits intervention to the natural speed of the epidemic, while immediate countrywide vaccination consistantly brings the epidemic to an end quickly. Note the variation in the localized strategies. The scenarios butting up against the right side of the frame were still active when the simulation was stopped at 500 days.

As expected, countrywide vaccination involves more vaccine-related deaths than do the localized strategies, though all strategies exhibit significant variation.

Ineffective quarantine is very expensive, largely as a result of the extended length of the epidemic. Countrywide vaccination is consistently the most expensive of the remaining options. Cost estimates are extremely rough. Use with caution.

Sensitivities

As expected, mortalities are strongly and positively affected by the mortality rate, the overall reproduction number, the time it takes to ID the outbreak, the length of the latency period, and the relative contagiousness of the prodromal period.

Mortality, however, is also highly sensitive to quarantine effectiveness, which is strongly correlated with the length of the latency period and the relative contagiousness of the prodromal period (as per model construction). While latency length and prodomal virulence are well-researched and documented, the coefficients on these variables in affecting quarantine effectiveness are not. It is left to the modeller to input them. Choosing coefficients that reflect more effective quarantine result, unsurprisingly, in the model recommending a strategy based on quarantine. As shown below, quarantine efficiencies of 70%-80% or above make quarantine/targetted vaccination the optimum strategy while an efficiency of 60% still leads to significant casualties. The effects are nonlinear.
